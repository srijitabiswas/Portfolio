LuxeStay Villas

A dual-sided rental platform designed through a research-first product approach

End-to-end product design · UX Strategy · Figma Prototyping

View Prototype

Overview

LuxeStay Villas was developed as part of the XPMC Xpro Program, focusing on building a complete product experience from research to final handoff. The goal was to design a seamless platform for both property owners and guests, ensuring clarity, trust, and usability.

Problem

Existing rental platforms often create friction between property owners and guests, with unclear communication, lack of trust, and inefficient booking flows.

Solution

Designed a dual-sided platform that simplifies interactions between owners and guests through structured flows, clear information hierarchy, and intuitive booking experiences.

Product Journey

  • Week 1 — Research: Identified user pain points and stakeholder needs
  • Week 2 — Planning: Defined scope, roadmap, and feature priorities
  • Week 3 — Design: Created UX flows and information architecture
  • Week 4 — Prototype: Built interactive Figma prototypes
  • Week 5 — Testing: Conducted usability testing and refined designs
  • Week 6 — Handoff: Delivered final designs and documentation

Key Features

  • Dual user flows (Owner & Guest)
  • Clear booking and listing experience
  • Trust-building UI elements
  • Structured navigation and hierarchy

My Role

  • Led UX decision-making and design direction
  • Designed user flows and interface layouts
  • Coordinated team efforts across sprints
  • Presented solutions to Product Owner

Outcome

Delivered a complete product experience with validated user flows, high-fidelity prototypes, and structured design documentation. The solution improved clarity in user journeys and established a scalable foundation for implementation.

Key Learnings

  • Clarity in thinking leads to better product decisions
  • UX is deeply connected to communication and collaboration
  • Structured workflows improve efficiency and outcomes

Acknowledgement

This project was part of the XPMC Xpro Program conducted by Employability.life in collaboration with Federation University Australia.
